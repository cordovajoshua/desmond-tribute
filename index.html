Honors and Recognition of Desmond Doss:

  • 1945 - The President of the United States of America, authorized by Act of Congress, March 3, 1863, has awarded in the name of The Congress the MEDAL OF HONOR to Private First Class Desmond T. Doss United States Army.
  • 1951 - Camp Desmond T. Doss was created in Grand Ledge, Michigan to help train young Seventh-day Adventist men for service in the military.
  • 1980 - A school in Lynchburg was renamed Desmond T. Doss Christian Academy. The school was founded by the Lynchburg Seventh-day Adventist Church, the home church of Desmond Doss during his years in Lynchburg.
  • 1990 - A section of Georgia Highway 2 between US Highway 27 and Georgia Highway 193 in Walker County was named the "Desmond T. Doss Medal of Honor Highway."
  • 2000 - Doss appeared before the Georgia House of Representatives and was presented a special resolution honoring his heroic accomplisments on behalf of the country.
  • 2004 - A statue of Doss was dedicated at the National Museum of Patriotism in Atlanta, Georgia.
  • 2007 - A statue of Doss was dedicated at Veterans Memorial Park in Collegedale, Tennessee.
  • 2008 - The guest house at Walter Reed Army Medical Center in Washington, D.C., was renamed Doss Memorial Hall.
  • 2008 - A two-mile stretch of Alabama Highway 9 in Piedmont was named the "Desmond T. Doss Sr." Memorial Highway.
  • 2016 - The city of Lyncburg, Virginia, awarded a plaque in his honor to Desmond T. Doss Christian Academy.
  • 2017 - PETA posthomously honored Doss with a Hero to Animals award in recognition of his lifelong commitment to vegetarianism.
  • 2020 - The Lynchburg Virginia Area Veterans Council dedicated a plaque at his former childhood home to commemorate the Desmond T. Doss Veterans Home.
